DC Motor Lesson

  • Motors convert electrical energy into mechanical energy through the use of electromagnetic fields, and rotating wire coils.
    • When a voltage is applied to a motor, it spins with some amount of speed at a proportional amount of torque.
      • Ex. 12 volts - X Speed 6 Y Torque
  • Motors apply torque in response to a load associated. Therefore, the motor will theoretically spin extremely fast with no load but we have to account for friction.
    • This is noted as the free speed.
  • When a motor is held and exerting all of the torque it can, we would say the motor is at stall.
    • When the speed is zero, we are at a point referred to as the stall torque
  • Speed and Torque are inversely proportional.
  • Current draw is proportional to the torque applied.

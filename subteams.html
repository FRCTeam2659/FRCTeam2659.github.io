Team Structure

The team is organized into three technical sub-teams and three management sub-teams, each with at least one students leader and one mentor. The technical sub-teams are in charge of designing, powering, and programming the robot whereas the business sub-teams are in charge of all team logistics. The tasks assigned to the management sub-teams include: logistics and outreach, finding and communicating with sponsors, scheduling, preparing all award submissions and presentations, completing the bill of materials, and handling all purchases and finances. The professional volunteers fulfill the roles of Team Lead Mentor, Lead Technical Mentor, and Lead Management Mentor. These roles oversee team administration and travel, the development of the robot, finances, and purchases. Prior to the build season, stidents fill out forms nominating themselves for leadership positions. These students are interviewed by the mentors and the mentors choose the students who they believe would best fill these leadership positions.

  • NOTE:

    The Lead Technical Mentor and Lead Management Mentor are restricted to only holding a single Lead Mentor position amongst the divided subteams of the RoboWarriors. However, the Lead Team Mentor may not be restricted to only that position. They may hold the Lead Technical Mentor or Lead Management Mentor position as well.

  • NOTE:

    One student may not hold two Student Leader positions on separate subteam divisions. Additionally, the Team Technical Captain and the Team Management Captain are not capable of holding a separate subteam Student Leader Position.

  • NOTE:

    This current structure is similar to many successful teams that are currently competing and that have in the past. While the above structure is defined as such above, it may shift due to student participation and resources available.

Future Structure

Among the non-competition and build portions of the season, there is also the competition aspect as well. At competition, there is a separate organizational chart that will be utilized to maximize our capability to use the skills learned during the previous portions of the season and be competitive.
    • Responsible for oversight of the entire RoboWarriors team at competition. Required to advise the Drive and Pit Mentor and Lead Strategy & Management Mentor with all pertinent information to maintain at a competitive level during the current competition.
    Responsible for oversight of the Drive Team and Pit Crew during competitions. Required to provide ample support to both teams that allow them to operate at the highest competitive capacity. Additionally, they will guide the other mentors and coordinate with the Lead Strategy & Management Mentor on conveying information between the two groups.
    Responsible for oversight of the Strategy Team and Management Team during competitions. Required to provide ample support to both teams that allow them to operate at the highest competitive capacity. Additionally, they will guide the other mentors and coordinate with the Lead Strategy & Management Mentor on conveying information between the two groups.
    Responsible for providing their technical or management skill set to enhance the competitive nature of the RoboWarriors during competitions. Required to provide detailed instruction and insight on specifics for any of the four subteams in order to assist their efforts. Additionally, all Mentors will be required to step into specific roles if vacancies in other areas occur.
    • Responsible for providing their acquired skills pertaining to being on a competitive drive team in order to help increase the team’s competitive nature at competitions. The Drive Team consists of a Driver, Operator, and Human Player(s) which all are chosen from the current students. Additionally, there is a Drive Coach that directs the Drive Team and that may be a Mentor or Student representative.
    Responsible for providing their technical skills to aid in robot maintenance and preparation between individual competition matches in order to stay at the team’s competitive level. The Pit Crew consists of the Pit Boss, two Pit Assistants, a Drive Assistance, and a Programmer / Safety Captain, which are all chosen from the current students.
    • Responsible for providing their technical and management skills to yield a strategical advantage for the RoboWarriors during competition. The Strategy Team consists of a Lead Strategist, Lead Scout, Strategist, and Scouts, which are all chosen from the current students.
    Responsible for providing their management skills to place the RoboWarriors in a positive light and provide team support during competition. The Management Team consists of two Awards representatives, Multimedia representatives, and Nutrition representatives, which are all chosen from the current students. They may be pulled from other assigned duties to help provide the proper support for other teams.
Rotational Students -

Responsible for upholding their role assigned during a specific time at the competition. All students placed on rotation are required to learn all aspects of the team during competition in order to fill future positions. While students are not in their specific rotation slot, they will be allowed to visit other locations at the competition to engage more with other teams or learn more about what’s offered through FIRST.

  • NOTE:

    The Lead Drive & Pit Mentor and Lead Strategy & Management Mentor are restricted to only holding a single lead position amongst the distinguished sections of the RoboWarriors during competitions. However, the Lead Competition Mentor may not be restricted to only that position. They may hold the Lead Drive & Pit Mentor or Lead Strategy & Management Mentor position as well.

  • NOTE:

    All students must participate during the competition portion of the season. If a student is not interested in being apart of the roles above then they will be shifted in a volunteer role to help benefit all teams that are competing.
